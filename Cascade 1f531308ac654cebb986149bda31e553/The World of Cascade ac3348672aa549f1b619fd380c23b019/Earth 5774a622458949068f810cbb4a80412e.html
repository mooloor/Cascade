Earth

Despite many attempts, Earth was never unified under a single pan-human government. No nation was willing to cede its sovereignty, despite the miniscule differences in ideology and culture. The powerful did not have much to do with the good, and, thus, Earth persists as a union of squabbling nationstates, with a small number of issues handled by the Earth Directorate, formed after the dissolution of the UN in the 21st century.

While planet-bound issues largely remain confined to the various countries, the situation is markedly different in orbit. The various rails in and out of Earth's gravity well are controlled solely by the Directorate, and the various Orbital States constantly fight for their independence in the face of the Directorate's meddling.

The reason for this is based mainly on Earth's position as the undisputed hegemon of the system's complex organic supply. If you want to grow plants in any kind of soil, that soil comes from Earth, and the directorate imposes draconian punishments on anyone circumventing the legal export process.

Several decades ago, the Earth Directorate Military was formed, with the stated intention of protecting humanity's homeworld from any external threats. It was this organisation that mounted the lightning raid on the Mercury Manufacturing Stations, seizing them for the Directorate under the charismatic leadership of Director James Habsburg.

The Major Nations of Earth:

The North American Union:

Technically not a nation, but a supranational organisation consisting of the United States of America and Canada, and the diplomatic arm under which both nations represent their interests.

The United Central American States:

In the mid 2100s, the Central American Federation was formed, an organisation, much like the EU, with the purpose of unifying the nations, easing trade barriers, and giving them greater pull on the world stage. Over time, this progressed to become a fully federal state, eventually dissolving the national borders altogether and resulting in the Union of Central American States.
