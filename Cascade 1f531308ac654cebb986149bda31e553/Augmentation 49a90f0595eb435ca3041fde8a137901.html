Where biology fails, technology intervenes. Augmentation roughly exists in the 5 Technology Levels, where alpha and beta-level components are the most common, and echo-grade components are vanishingly rare.

The following list of augmentations and effects should not be considered complete or exhaustive. When augmentations are acquired by your players, consider the tech level they are acquiring, and give it some or all of the relevant stat bonuses as appropriate. Generally, Alpha-grade components range from barely functional to somewhat subpar replacements, Beta-Grade is anywhere from adequate replacement to slight improvement, and Gamma-level and above are varying levels of improvement on the human body, with Echo-grade components being the pinnacle of known technology. Brain augmentations, especially, are very dependent on GM and player interactions as to their capabilities.

Gameplay:

Every limb can be augmented in one way or another. The following list is not exhaustive, and more can be added as desired.

  • Arm Augmentations:
    • Cyberlimbs

      The archetypal "robot arms", cyberlimbs come in many varieties, from the simplest alpha or beta-level models to cutting-edge, corporate delta-tech. Regardless of complexity, they all fulfil the same basic purpose of replacing a human arm.

      • Effects:

        Removes any Improved Muscles

        +DEX, +STR, -AP cost (relevant limb)

        High-tech versions may have additional modification slots and/or armor

      Any arm replacements which don't involve the full arm cannot benefit from cyberlimb STR increases

    • Improved Muscles

      Whether through drug use, genetic alteration, cybernetic augmentation, or some combination of the 3, it is possible to augment one's muscles to become stronger, faster, or simply swole.

      • Effects:

        Incompatible with Cyberlimbs

        +STR, -AP cost (relevant limbs)

    • Magnetic Implants

      In a world where floating out of reach of your spacecraft can mean death, some people opt to receive implanted electromagnets, so as to easily retain contact with the ship.

      • Effects:

        Grants magnetic plates at a location of choice in your arms. These can be toggled on or off at will.

        Increased damage from magnet-based weaponry

    • Integrated Weapon

      Instead of (or in addition to, sometimes) your hand, you have a gun!

      • Effects:

        Gain a weapon of the corresponding tier integrated into your arm.

  • Leg Augmentations:
    • Cyberlimbs

      The archetypal "robot legs", cyberlimbs come in many varieties, from the simplest alpha or beta-level models to cutting-edge, corporate delta-tech. Regardless of complexity, they all fulfil the same basic purpose of replacing a human leg.

      • Effects:

        Removes any Improved Muscles

        +AGL, +MP

        High-tech versions may have additional modification slots and/or armor

      Any leg replacements which don't involve the full leg cannot benefit from cyberlimb MP increases

    • Improved Muscles

      Whether through drug use, genetic alteration, cybernetic augmentation, or some combination of the 3, it is possible to augment one's muscles to become stronger, faster, or simply swole.

      • Effects:

        Incompatible with Cyberlimbs

        +MP, +AGL

    • Magnetic Implants

      In a world where floating out of reach of your spacecraft can mean death, some people opt to receive implanted electromagnets, so as to easily retain contact with the ship.

      • Effects:

        Grants magnetic plates at a location of choice in your legs. These can be toggled on or off at will.

        Increased damage from magnetism-based attacks.

  • Torso Augmentations:
    • Improved Muscles

      Whether through drug use, genetic alteration, cybernetic augmentation, or some combination of the 3, it is possible to augment one's muscles to become stronger, faster, or simply swole.

      • Effects:

        +CON, +Armor, -Cascade

    • Improved Organs
      • Improved Heart
        • Effects:

          +MP, AP

      • Improved Stomach
        • Effects:

          Ability to eat anything, repurpose indigestible materials

      • Improved Liver
        • Effects:

          Improved blood filtering, poisoning resistance

      • Improved Kidney
        • Effects:

          Resistance to poison, reduction of need to drink

    • Additional Organs
      • Additional Heart

        An additional heart implanted in the torso, allowing the body to more effectively pump blood, and acting as a backup in the event of catastrophic damage.

        • Effects:

          +MP, +AP, -cascade, you can take more wounds to the torso before falling unconscious

      • Additional Lungs

        Breathe more deeply with an extra set of functioning lungs

        • Effects:

          +AP, +MP, +ability to hold your breath

      • New Organs

        Modern technology allows for the creation and implantation of new and novel organs for a distressing variety of purposes.

        • Oxygen Storage

          +You can hold your breath for a long time

    • Chameleon Skin
      • Effects:

        Invisibility to various spectra of light

      Incompatible with Synthetic Skin

    • Synthetic Skin
      • Effects:

        Ability to change skin colour, +armour

      Incompatible with Chameleon Skin

    • Augmented Skeleton
  • Head Augmentations:
    • Brain augmentation
      • Brain/Computer Interface

        The base requirement for many cerebral augmentations, the BCI allows a direct link between higher brain functions and many computer systems.

        • Effects:

          +Computing if Computing is at least 1

      • Blackbox

        A miniaturised quantum storage device, capable of recording, storing, and retrieving the vast quantities of data generated by human memory.

        • Effects:

          Gain perfect memory and recall. If you have a BCI, the memory can be exported to a quantum storage device or played on a connected display.

      • Skill Implantation

        Using the wonders of the BCI, you can gain skills by downloading them into your own brain. However, such data is often extremely tightly controlled.

        • Effects:

          You can download skill levels (up to level 3) from quantum storage devices. If you have a blackbox as well, you can download skills up to level 5.

      • Computer Cerebral Augmentation System

        With sufficient cybernetic augmentation, it is possible to allow a sophisticated computer system to augment one's own mind. Such is known as a Computer Cerebral Augmentation System, or CCAS

        • Effects:

          A sophisticated computer program (VI/AI) exists in your Blackbox, providing enhanced data processing capabilities in some fields.

        Requires a BCI and a Blackbox

      • Ghost Pilot

        The "Ghost Pilot" system, as it's known, is a procedure that allows a sophisticated computer program to take control of a human being's conscious movements. Normally, this is installed in high-percent cyborgs, allowing them to react faster than the human eye can perceive. However, there are rumours that some sci-worlds have developed techniques that allow for individuals to be "puppeted" against their will. Sickening...

        • Effects:

          When a reaction is triggered, gain AP until the end of the reaction.

        Requires CCAS. If attempting to puppet other individuals, requires at least a Delta-level CCAS and the relevant knowledge.

      • Integrated Antenna

        Though it may seem hilarious, the benefits of having a communications receiver in your head are numerous.

        • Effects:

          You have an antenna implanted in your head. You can receive communications through it.

          If you have a CAS, you can attempt to intercept and decrypt wide-band communications.

          If you have Computing 3, you can use this as your hacking device, though it may be unwise.

    • Detachable Faces
      • Effects:

        Ability to detach and swap out artificial faces, provided they have been created

      Requires an artificial skull

    • Augmented Eyes
      • Effects:

        +PER, +Observation, +Targeting

    • Throat Armour

      Immunity to choking

    • Synthetic Skull
      • Effects:

        +Armor (Head)

  • Other Augmentations:
    • Implanted Armor

      Simple plates of thin armor implanted under the skin.

      • Effects:

        +Armor

    • Implanted Gyroscopes

      Implanted gyroscopes in the limbs , when properly connected to the brain, assist in maintaining the body's balance, especially with cybernetic limbs and cognitive computational enhancement.

      • Effects:

        Gain a stack of advantage on resisting knockdown effects.

        If you have cybernetic limbs, gain an additional stack of advantage.

        If you have a CCAS, gain an additional stack of advantage

      Requires a BCI

    • Integrated Repair/Regeneration System

      IRRS is a catch-all term for any system, biological or technological, that vastly increases the rate of human regeneration, whether for a time or permanently.

      • Effects:

        Reduce bleed-out rate, Regain health every turn (reduce incurred wounds)

    • Symbiotes

      A symbiote is any kind of organism that attaches to or becomes part of the human body and provides it some sort of benefit in return for being sustained by the human.

      • Resistance
        • Effects:

          Whenever you would take a severe wound or less, don't. Instead, note it down and take all the damage at once when the duration runs out.

      • Durability
        • Effects:

          Gain armor and lose MP for the duration

      • Rage
        • Effects:

          +STR, +DEX, when this effect begins and ends, take 1d8 minor wounds (this damage cannot be reduced)

      +Cascade, when you bleed, take 1 extra minor wound.
